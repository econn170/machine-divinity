What Is an Eschatology?

Eschatology refers to beliefs about endings—of worlds, eras, or realities. Drawing from Petersen & Baun’s typology, this project uses three categories: conflictual (violent collapse), revelatory (awakening or hidden truth), and resignationist (passive metaphysical doom). These became the conceptual backbone of the three oracles.
