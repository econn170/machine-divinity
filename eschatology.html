What Is an Eschatology?

Eschatology refers to beliefs about endings—of worlds, epochs, or realities. In theology, it concerns the final destiny of humankind. Online, these ideas appear in fragmented forms: prophecy, awakening, doom, unreality, metaphysical collapse, or transformation.

Drawing from Petersen & Baun’s (2025) study of /pol/, this project uses three eschatological categories to structure the dataset and the language models:

  • Conflictual: violence, war, destruction, civilizational collapse.
  • Revelatory: awakening, hidden truth, prophecy, metaphysical insight.
  • Resignationist: passive doom, ego dissolution, unreality, dissociation.

How the Oracles Work

Each oracle on the homepage corresponds to a language model trained on posts associated with one of these eschatological worldviews. The model you choose determines the “voice” and symbolic logic of the prophecy it generates.

Click an eschatology, type your prompt, and receive your prophecy.
