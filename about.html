Machine Divinity About Process Ethics README

About the Project

Machine Divinity is a computational-artistic exploration of mystical, apocalyptic, and psychosis-coded language across anonymous online spaces. The project analyzes 4chan discourse through an eschatological framework and trains three generative “oracles,” each representing a distinct linguistic worldview.

On the homepage, users can choose between three oracles—revelatory, conflictual, or resignationist. These categories are drawn from Petersen & Baun’s (2025) study of the /pol/ board, where they identified three eschatological formations tied to different conceptions of agency, crisis, and symbolic meaning.

What Is an Eschatology?

Eschatology refers to beliefs about endings, or “the part of theology concerned with death, judgment, and the final destiny of the soul and of humankind.” Petersen & Baun’s analysis of 4chan posts (2014–2023) found a rise in eschatological worldviews, which they argue reflects “ideological adaptations to the increasing complexity and scope of societal issues, as perceived by the community.”

While this project began as an exploration of “spiritual” language on 4chan more broadly, the eschatological framework offered a compelling structure through which to interpret emergent forms of digital mysticism, revelation, and doom.

What Do the Buttons Do?

Each oracle button corresponds to a language model trained on posts classified within one of the three eschatological categories. When you choose an oracle, you are selecting its worldview:

  • Conflictual eschatology: violent transformation, war, destruction.
  • Revelatory eschatology: awakening, hidden truths, prophecy, metaphysical revelation.
  • Resignationist eschatology: passive metaphysical doom, dissociation, ego death.

Choose an eschatology, write your prompt, and receive your prophecy.
